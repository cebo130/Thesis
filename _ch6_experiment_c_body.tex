\chapter{Experiment C: Longitudinal Feedback Dynamics}
\label{ch:exp_c}

\section{Purpose and alignment with the research design}
Experiment~C addresses \textbf{RQ3}: how popularity- and diversity-related metrics evolve when a recommender is repeatedly retrained on logs that are themselves produced by the system. It tests \textbf{Hypothesis~H3} from Chapter~\ref{ch:lit_review}: under iterative retraining with synthetic clicks drawn from a mixed relevance--popularity rule, collaborative trajectories should exhibit \emph{rising} ARP and \emph{contracting} aggregate diversity, whereas TF--IDF trajectories should be flatter or recovering because item--text similarity does not require dense co-occurrence refresh \citep{chaney2018algorithmic, mansoury2020feedback}. Metrics and the click model are defined in Chapter~\ref{ch:methodology}, Sections~\ref{sec:method_metrics_rq3} and~\ref{sec:method_click}. As noted there, findings are \textbf{descriptive}: each model--dataset path is a single realised trajectory, not a distribution over seeds.

\section{Simulation protocol}
The implementation in \texttt{run\_all.py} proceeds as follows (see also Chapter~\ref{ch:methodology} for notation):
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Initial log.} Starting from the $k$-core filtered corpus (Chapter~\ref{ch:methodology}), an \textbf{80\%} pseudo-random subsample of all interaction rows forms $\mathcal{D}_0$ for both arms (\texttt{train\_test\_split(..., test\_size=0.2, random\_state=42)} on the full rating table; the held-out $20\%$ is not consumed inside the loop). The same user set is threaded through all rounds.
  \item \textbf{Models.} Only \textbf{SVD} (biased matrix factorisation via Surprise) and \textbf{TF--IDF} profile recommendations are simulated, trained afresh on the current log each round.
  \item \textbf{Recommendations.} Each active user receives the top-\textbf{five} unseen items under the model's scoring rule; SVD uses min--max normalised predicted ratings as relevance, TF--IDF uses cosine scores on TF--IDF vectors built from the current training catalogue.
  \item \textbf{Clicks.} For each recommended slot, a Bernoulli draw uses $\mathrm{clip}(0.7\,\mathrm{Rel}+0.3\,\tilde{P},[0,1])$ with $\tilde{P}$ the min--max normalised training popularity in the \emph{current} log (Section~\ref{sec:method_click}). Accepted clicks append a rating of $5.0$; duplicate $(u,i)$ pairs keep the latest event.
  \item \textbf{Horizon.} Rounds $t=1,\ldots,5$ repeat training $\rightarrow$ recommendation $\rightarrow$ augmentation for each model on its own growing log.
\end{enumerate}
Public datasets use the shared loaders; Takealot uses the reviews-derived split with the same code path via \texttt{run\_takealot\_full.py}.

\section{Empirical patterns}
\textbf{MovieLens 1M (SVD).} The clearest illustration of H3 is MovieLens~1M under SVD: $\mathrm{ARP}$ climbs from $\approx 893$ to $\approx 1997$ while aggregate diversity \emph{falls} from $649$ to $529$ unique items despite the log growing the recommender concentrates on an ever-smaller effective support even as synthetic clicks accumulate. TF--IDF on the same corpus moves in the opposite direction on diversity ($805 \rightarrow 1278$) while ARP rises more modestly ($348 \rightarrow 584$), consistent with metadata-driven exploration that is less tightly coupled to co-occurrence frequency alone.

\textbf{MovieLens 100K.} SVD's ARP still rises strongly ($165 \rightarrow 406$) with modest oscillation in $\mathrm{AD}_t$; TF--IDF increases both ARP and diversity ($235 \rightarrow 394$), matching the ``resilience'' pattern posited in H3 at this density.

\textbf{Last.fm 2K.} SVD shows a dramatic ARP increase ($\approx 177 \rightarrow 700$) with only a small net change in aggregate diversity ($170 \rightarrow 166$), indicating that popularity mass shifts without a large change in the count of distinct surfaced items. TF--IDF grows both metrics moderately, reflecting the binned implicit--to--explicit ratings and artist-tag signal.

\textbf{Book-Crossing.} SVD \emph{expands} aggregate diversity ($134 \rightarrow 715$) while \emph{reducing} ARP ($33 \rightarrow 24$). This configuration shows that the feedback loop is not a universal monotonicity machine: when the initial log is extremely sparse and head-heavy, additional clicks can reintroduce long-tail mass into the training graph and temporarily diversify recommendations even as mean popularity drifts downward. TF--IDF instead follows the more familiar pattern of rising diversity and ARP.

\textbf{Takealot.} On the proprietary graph, SVD's ARP explodes from $\approx 12$ to $\approx 414$ over five rounds while aggregate diversity still increases ($3196 \rightarrow 4825$) because the absolute number of unique recommendations grows with the augmented log; interpreted alongside Experiment~A's near-degenerate one-shot ALS coverage, the ARP path is the stronger signature of popularity creep. TF--IDF keeps ARP in single digits through round~5 ($\approx 6$) with nearly flat diversity ($\approx 1.67\times 10^{4}$ items), indicating that textual similarity anchors exploration even as clicks accrete.

\section{Limitations and non-inferential status}
The simulator uses a single random seed, a fixed $\alpha=0.7$ click blend, and a five-round horizon. Chapter~\ref{ch:methodology} therefore treats trajectories as \textbf{case studies} rather than samples from a generative process: no $p$-values are attached to slopes. Sensitivity to $\alpha$, to the top-$K$ choice (five slots per user per round), and to alternative click rules (e.g.\ purely propensity-thresholded acceptance) remains open work.

\section{Discussion and answer to RQ3}
\textbf{RQ3 longitudinal dynamics:} On the densest movie benchmark (MovieLens~1M), SVD exhibits the canonical pattern of rising ARP with falling aggregate diversity under synthetic feedback, whereas TF--IDF increases diversity with a gentler ARP slope, supporting H3 in the regime where collaborative co-occurrence signal is abundant. Smaller corpora and the music domain show mixed monotonicity, and Book-Crossing under SVD inverts the diversity trend, underscoring that feedback effects are \emph{path-dependent}. Takealot highlights ARP growth under SVD even when diversity counts rise, motivating joint reading of Chapters~\ref{ch:exp_a}--\ref{ch:exp_c} for e-commerce stress tests.
